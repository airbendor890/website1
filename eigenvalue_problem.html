Eigen value problem

Power Iteration/Von Mises Method

  • A $ \in R^{d\times d}$ is Diagonalizable.
  • Let $\lambda_1,\lambda_2,.....,\lambda_d$ be d eigenvalues of A and Let $v_1,v_2,......,v_d$ be Corresponding eigenvectors.
  • Let $b_0=c_1v_1+c_2v_2+.......+c_dv_d$ such that $c_1 \neq 0$.
  • Suppose $\lambda_1$ is the dominant eigenvalue such that $|\lambda_1|\gt |\lambda_j|$ for j$\gt$ 1.
  • $A^kb_0=c_1A^kv_1+c_2A^kv_2+.......+c_dA^kv_d$ $=c_1\lambda_1^kv_1+c_2\lambda_2^kv_2+.......+c_d\lambda_d^kv_d$
    $=c_1\lambda_1^k\left[ v_1+\frac{c_2}{c_1}(\frac{\lambda_2}{\lambda_1})^kv_2 +.......+(\frac{\lambda_d}{\lambda_1})^kv_d\right]$
  • $|\frac{\lambda_j}{\lambda_1}|\lt 1$ for j$\gt$ 1.Therefore $A^kb_0$ $\rightarrow$ $c_1\lambda_1^kv_1$ AS $k \rightarrow \infty$
  • $b_k=\frac{A^kb_0}{||A^kb_0||}$ converges to eigen vector of a corresponding to maximum eigen value.
  • $\mu_k=\frac{b_k^TAb_k}{b_k^Tb_k}$ converges to dominant eigen value.
  1. Produce random vector $b_0$
  2. While(k$\lt$NO_of_loops)
  3.   $b_{k+1}=Ab_k$
  4.   $\mu_k=\frac{b_k^Tb_{k+1}}{b_k^Tb_k}$
  5.   $b_k=\frac{b_{k+1}}{||b_{k+1}||}$
  6. End While
  7. Return $\mu_k$(largest eigen value) ,$b_k$(corresponding eigen vector).
